Change Strategy

Your Strategy

Designing and implementing an effective change strategy is an agile, iterative process. The aim (and how you know you’ve been successful!) is high levels of end-user adoption, confidence, and usage that meet stated outcomes, be they related to a new system, process, reorg, or cultural shift.

People Side of Change

Change impacts people. People - as we know - are unique, dynamic, and can change their minds from day-to-day. They are also notoriously resistant to changes they don’t understand, feel connected to, or see the value of. A meaningful and successful change strategy supports the people-side of change with a defined and dynamic (read: evolving) framework within the context it operates.

Key Elements

The key elements to consider - and include - in a results-oriented change strategy are as follows:

  • Sponsorship & Governance Model: who is championing this change? Who are the active and visible supporters? How engaged/available are they able to be?
  • Communication: who are the audiences that need to be communicated with about this initiative? What are the key messages? How are they being communicated, by whom, and in what way?
  • Training/Education: what skills/knowledge do impacted end-users need to be confident and successful in the "new world?"" How do we provide that for them?
  • Coaching: how comfortable/adept are your change leaders leading change? What support/coaching do they need to be able to advocate and sustain change, both for themselves and their teams?
  • Resistance Management: how well do you know your end-users? How prepared are you to hear their questions, comments, and concerns about the change? Who are your biggest resistors? Do you know what they need?
  • Measurement: how do we know if we’ve been successful? How are people feeling? What are the stories people are telling about the change?
